TIC-TAC-TOE

About:

Tic-Tac-Toe is a classic two-player board game that is played on a 3x3 grid.

Objective: The main objective of the game is to form a line of three of your own symbols (either X or O) horizontally, vertically, or diagonally on the game board.

Player Turns: The game is played by two players, typically denoted as X and O, who take turns marking the spaces on the grid.

Grid Structure: The game board consists of a 3x3 grid, providing a total of 9 spaces for the players to place their symbols.

Symbol Identification: One player uses the "X" symbol, and the other player uses the "O" symbol. Players take turns placing their symbol in an empty space on the grid.

Quick Gameplay: The simplicity and quick nature of the game make it accessible for players of all ages.

How to Play:

Tic-Tac-Toe is a two-player board game played on a 3x3 grid.

The objective is to form a line of three of your symbols (X or O) horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Players take turns marking empty spaces with their respective symbols.

One player uses "X," and the other uses "O."

The game starts with the player using "X," and turns alternate.

Once a space is marked, it cannot be occupied by the opposing player.

The game ends when a player successfully aligns three of their symbols in a row.

If the entire grid is filled with no winner, the game is a draw.
